Long-duration meeting transcription systems have become increasingly prevalent in both enterprise and professional settings. While speech-to-text accuracy has improved substantially, effective and efficient retrieval of information from lengthy transcripts remains a significant challenge. Meadow9+, a long-meeting transcription application developed by ABAX AI, recently introduced a chatbot feature to address this need; however, its current implementation processes entire transcripts directly as input to a large language model (LLM), resulting in high latency, excessive computational cost, and degraded answer quality due to long-context limitations.

This project proposes and develops a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)--based system to optimize transcript retrieval and chatbot performance for Meadow9+. By embedding transcript segments into a vector space and retrieving only the most relevant contextual chunks for each user query, the proposed system significantly reduces inference overhead while preserving answer accuracy. In addition, this work introduces a comprehensive evaluation pipeline that systematically assesses both retrieval quality and end-to-end question-answering performance using metrics such as Hit Rate, Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR), F1 score, and overall accuracy, alongside detailed latency benchmarking.

Experimental results demonstrate that the RAG-based chatbot achieves comparable accuracy to the existing full-transcript LLM approach while delivering substantial improvements in response time, validating the effectiveness of retrieval-driven optimization. Beyond immediate chatbot acceleration, the embedding-based architecture enables extensibility toward more advanced retrieval techniques, including phonetic search, fuzzy matching, and hybrid retrieval strategies tailored to noisy speech-to-text outputs. This report presents the system design, implementation progress, and evaluation results to date, and outlines future work focused on building a comprehensive, efficient, and deployable retrieval system.
